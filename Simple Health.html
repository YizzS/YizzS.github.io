Exercise Everywhere in Life

In the Office.

Back of the Legs Stretch

If you don’t sit properly you could be reducing the ability for blood to circulate properly, especially in your legs.

Instructions

While sitting, extend your legs and fold your body and reach down towards your toes. Make sure to keep your back straight.

Read More

Chair Back Stretch

All you need is the back of a chair.You get to stretch the back of your thighs and your low back at the same time.

Instructions

Stand up straight with a slight knee bend and your arms above your head. Begin the stretch by bending at the waist while keeping your feet flat on the ground, back straight, and arms above the head.

Read More

Desk Thighs Flexibility Balance

When you spend all day sitting down, you lose a lot of your mobility and balance.

Instructions

Using the desk for support, stand up and raise your leg behind you, grabbing hold of the ankle (or your shin if you cant quite reach). Lift the leg as high as you can keeping your knee bent at a right angle.

Read More

Standing While Working

Standing at your desk at work helps offset all that sitting time and allows you to work on your strength and posture.

Instructions

Its important not to do too much at one time so work your way up to a full day of standing with an hour or two at a time. Also make sure your computer and keyboard are at a good height so you aren't straining your eyes or your neck with bad posture.

In the Bathroom.

Drop The Soap

You’ll stretch tight hamstrings while also working your glutes.Take it slow since you’re starting from a squat, barefoot in a slippery wet shower. Drop the Soap might have a slightly odd look to it, but, nobody is looking, right?

Instructions

Squat down with your knees bent and arch your back. Put your fingers under your toes of both feet and grip your toes.

Toothbrush Calf Raises

Can we agree that well-shaped calf muscles make you look great in your running shorts? How about with that cocktail dress and those new heels?

Instructions

Stand square in front of the mirror with your feet flat on the floor and your tailbone tucked in. You can place one hand on the counter if you need to for balance. Lift your heels off the floor; stand on your tippy toes.

Arm Stretch On The Can

It’s best known as a great stretch to warm things up before exercise, but it’s also a perfect way to relieve the day’s stress and anxiety while you’re seated on the can.

Instructions

Lift one of your arms over your head, positioning the bicep several inches away from your ear and hang your forearm behind you. Grasp your elevated elbow with the other hand.

Toothbrush Squats

Well, for starters you’ll have really clean teeth. We’re talking about squats here, so it’s a bit of a stretch to think we can make them fun.

Instructions

Stand with your feet turned slightly outward under your hips. Squeeze your buttocks. Now, slowly bend your knees to lower your trunk just a few inches and keep your feet flat on the floor.

On the Move.

Airplane Chest Stretch

Use this simple chest stretch in your seat to stretch your chest and reduce the tension you feel in your chest and you will be getting off the plane revitalized and ready to go about your day!

Instructions

Sit forward in your seat and put your arms behind you, palms flat on the seat. Look up slightly and open your chest up towards the ceiling.

Sitting Commuter Pec Stretch

People usually aren't happy while sitting in traffic on their way home from work but you will be if you stretch those pecs while you are waiting for traffic to clear.

Instructions

You might need to open up your window for this one and you probably only want to do it while the car is stopped at a light. Bring your arms out to the side and bring your chest up and out in front of you.

Standing Train Commute

"There is nothing better than riding the train with everyone else in the morning" - no one ever. That being said, riding the train and doing it in standing helps with any number of things including blood pressure regulation, balance and endurance.

Instructions

A simple exercise but something that can be pretty challenging: Ride the train and do it while standing.

One Legged Squat

The one-legged squat helps to reduce spinal compression and knee pain while improving your balance and core stability.

Instructions

With your arms in front of you, plant your dominant leg and raise the other 6" off the floor. Bend at the knee, drive your hips backward and descend until your butt touches the stairs.
